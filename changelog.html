Before:

Separate materials address caregiving and medical conditions in my aunt’s final period. They describe an absence of appropriate dementia care, infection-control practices, and professional support, alongside admissions by the caregivers that basic hygiene and in-home care were not being maintained. The medical sequence culminating in recurrent C. difficile, sepsis, and hospital-acquired pneumonia is connected to those conditions and offered as evidence of medical neglect. This dimension matters because it shows motive, opportunity, and the functional dependence that made legal “consents” impossible in reality.

After:

Separate materials address caregiving and medical conditions in my aunt’s final period. They describe an absence of appropriate dementia care, infection-control practices, and professional support, alongside admissions that essential caregivers were not reasonably employed to provide basic hygiene and in-home care, which were not being maintained based on the septic nature of the recurring infections that ultimately resulted in my aunt's death. The medical sequence culminating in recurrent C. difficile, sepsis, and hospital-acquired pneumonia is connected to those conditions and offered as evidence of medical neglect. This dimension matters because it shows motive, opportunity, and the functional dependence that made legal “consents” impossible in reality.
